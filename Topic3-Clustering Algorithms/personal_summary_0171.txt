Topics Covered in Topic 2    Probability and Statistics basic definitions  Event and sample space of a coin toss  Sample space = {head, tail}; event = {{head}, {tail}}  Event and sample space of a dice roll  Sample space = {1,2,3,4,5,6}; event = {{1},{2},{3},{4},{5},{6}}    Probability, if P(A) is the probability of Event A, then 1 - P(A) is the probability that the event  does not occur. Joint Probability, the probability of two events happening together and is denoted as    P(A and B) or P(A) x P(B)    Conditional Probability, the probability of some event A given the occurrence of another  event B. Data wrangling or data munging, is the process of cleaning, transforming, and organizing a    dataset to make it suitable for analysis. Image as data concept, by dividing the image into smaller blocks and computing feature of choice (i.e. average color, brightness, shapes)    Text data representation, using a bag of words to represent text that describes the occurrence of words feeding a document. By turning the words into vectors then into feature matrix, two documents can be compared.    Scaling, the process of converting a set of values to a new range of values   Normalization, scaling technique used to transform the values of a dataset into a common  range.
